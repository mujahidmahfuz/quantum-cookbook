The cookbook

The chapters above teach things in order, building each idea on the last. The Cookbook is the opposite: a flat, searchable set of short recipes for exactly when you already know what you need, mid-problem-set, and just want the answer fast — "how do I build a Bell pair," "how do I check if a set of Kraus operators is complete," that kind of thing. No lecture-style buildup, no prerequisites, just the minimal code or circuit and a paragraph of why it works. Several recipes already exist scattered across the chapters that reference them (Ch. 5's tensor-product worked examples, Ch. 11's channel presets); a standalone, fully indexed Cookbook page pulling all of those into one searchable place is the next thing coming online.
